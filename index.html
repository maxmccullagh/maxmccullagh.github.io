About

The pursuit
of photons

I focus on wide-field imaging of nebulae and galaxies — the kind of deep-sky objects that demand patience, dark skies, and precise equipment. Based in the Pacific Northwest, I regularly image from my light polluted front yard, but also have a remotely operated telescope under some of the darkest skies in the country.

My workflow spans every stage: target planning, polar alignment, calibration frame acquisition, stacking in PixInsight, and final processing. I'm comfortable with both broadband and narrowband (Ha, OIII, SII) imaging, and increasingly interested in the engineering side of the hobby.

Beyond the camera, I build custom 3D-printed hardware to improve my imaging setup. I care deeply about the quality of my equipment, and strive to optimize every aspect of my workflow.

Workflow

From photons
to pixels

01
Target selection

Stellarium, SkySafari, and moon phase planning. Object elevation, angular size, and seasonal availability all factor into what goes on the imaging list.

02
Acquisition

Remote sessions from dark sites. N.I.N.A. for sequencing, PHD2 for guiding. Calibration frames — darks, flats, bias — collected for every session.

03
Integration

PixInsight for stacking and calibration. WBPP for preprocessing. Multi-night data integrated across sessions with rejection algorithms tuned per target.

04
Processing

BlurXTerminator, NoiseXTerminator, StarXTerminator. Careful histogram stretching, colour calibration with SPCC, and masked curve adjustments.
